Abstraction heuristic induced by abstraction - Definition  	[latex]Let $\Pi$ be an FDR planning task with state space $S$ and let $\mathcal{A}$ be an abstraction of $\mathcal{T)(\Pi)$ with mapping $\alpha$.\\ \\The abstraction heuristic induced by $ \mathcal{A)$ and $\alpha, h^{\mathcal{A}, \alpha}$ is the heuristic function $h^{ \mathcal{A}, \alpha} : S\to \mathbb{N}_0 \cup \{ \infty \}$ which maps each state $s \in S$ to $h_{\mathcal{A}}^{*}(\alpha(s))$ (goal distance of $\alpha(s)$ in $ \mathcal{A}$)[/latex]
Abstraction Heuristic induced by strict homomorphism  	[latex]Let $\Pi$ be a FDR planning task and $\alpha$ a strict homomorphism on $\mathcal{T}(\Pi)$. The abstraction heuristic induced by $\alpha, h^{\alpha}$ isthe abstraction heuristic induced by $ \mathcal{T}(\Pi)^{\alpha}$ and$\alpha$, i.e.:\begin{equation*}h^{\alpha} := h^{ \mathcal{T}(\Pi)^{\alpha}, \alpha}\end{equation*}[/latex]
[latex]Theorem: Consistency and admissibility of $h^{ \mathcal{A}, \alpha }$[/latex] 	[latex]Let $\Pi$ be FDR planning task, let $ \mathcal{A} $ be abstraction of $\mathcal{T}(\Pi)$ with mapping $\alpha$.\\Then $h^{ \mathcal{A}, \alpha }$ is safe, goal-aware, admissible and consistent.[/latex]
Orthogonality of abstraction mappings - Definition  	[latex]Let $\alpha_1, alpha_2$ be abstraction mappings on $ \mathcal{T}$.\\\\$\alpha_1$ and $\alpha_2$ are orthogonal if for all transition $\langle s,l,t\rangle $ of $ \mathcal{T}$, we have $\alpha_{i}(s) = \alpha_{i}(t)$ for atleast one $i \in \{1,2\}$.[/latex]
Affecting transition labels - Definition  	[latex]Let $ \mathcal{T}$ be a transition system and $l$ be a label of $ \mathcal{T}$.We say that $l$ affects $ \mathcal{T}$ if $ \mathcal{T} $ has a transition $\langle s,l,t \rangle$ with $s \neq t$.[/latex]
Theorem - Affecting labels vs orthogonality  	[latex]Let $ \mathcal{A}_1, \mathcal{A}_2$ be abstractions of $ \mathcal{T}$ withmappings $\alpha_1, \alpha_2$ respectively.\\ \\If no label of $ \mathcal{T}$ affects both $ \mathcal{A}_1$ and $\mathcal{A}_2$ then $\alpha_1$ and $\alpha_2$ are orthogonal.[/latex]
Orthogonality of abstraction mappings and additivity - Theorem 	[latex]Let $h^{ \mathcal{A}_1, \alpha_1 }, \dots, h^{ \mathcal{A}_n, \alpha_n }$ beabstraction heuristics for the same task $\Pi$ s.t. $\alpha_i, \alpha_j$ orthogonal for all $i \neq j$.\\ \\Then $\sum \limits_{i=1}^{n} h^{ \mathcal{A}_i, \alpha_i}$ is a safe,goal-aware, admissible and consistent heuristic for $\Pi$.[/latex]

Comparison: Isomorphism vs graph equivalence  	[latex]\begin{itemize}\item $(\sim)$ and $(\stackrel{G}{\sim})$ are equivalence relations\item Two Isomorphic transition systems are completely interchangeable\item Two graph-equivalent transition systems are interchangeable most of thetime. Examples of Bad Abstractions: One-State Heuristic, Identity Abstraction,Perfect Abstraction - How are they and why are they bad? 	[latex]\begin{itemize}\item One-state Abstraction:\\$\alpha(s) := const$\\Pro: few abstract states and succinct encoding for $\alpha$\\Con: completely uninformative heuristic\item Identity Abstraction:\\$\alpha(s) := s$\\Pro: perfect heuristic and succinct encoding for $\alpha$\\Con: too many abstract states\item Perfect abstraction:\\$\alpha(s)=h^*(s)$\\Pro: perfect heuristic, few abstract states\\Con: no succinct encoding for $\alpha$\end{itemize}[/latex]
